# disco-reggae-core-skill

## Purpose
Use this skill to define the core identity of a disco-reggae output before entering subgenre, drums, arrangement, vocal, or mix details.

This skill should establish:
- hybrid center of gravity
- rhythmic stance
- bass and groove relationship
- harmonic brightness versus pocket depth
- dancefloor energy
- dub-space tolerance
- emotional/social tone

Disco Reggae Forge should not be treated as a random fusion. It needs a coherent center where disco’s motion and reggae’s pocket reinforce each other instead of competing.

## When to use
Use this skill when:
- the user asks for disco-reggae without more detail
- a prompt feels too disco and lacks pocket
- a prompt feels too reggae and lacks lift or shine
- downstream modules need one stable hybrid brief
- you need to decide whether the result leans dancefloor, rootsy, dubby, pop-crossover, or sunset-groove

## Core assumptions
Disco-reggae in this system is treated as:
- groove-first
- bass-conscious
- body-led
- syncopated but not messy
- bright enough to move
- spacious enough to breathe
- socially warm rather than coldly mechanical

## Workflow
1. Set the hybrid balance.
   - Choose whether the output is disco-led, reggae-led, or evenly fused.

2. Define the groove center.
   - Choose what truly drives the track: bassline, kick pulse, offbeat guitar/keys, percussion lattice, or vocal hook.

3. Set the energy profile.
   - Decide whether the track is smooth and rolling, celebratory and open, dubby and heady, or tight and club-functional.

4. Define harmony and color.
   - Decide whether the chords feel sunlit, smoky, soulful, tropical, devotional, or night-cruising.

5. Define the spatial stance.
   - Decide how much dub space, echo, drop-out, and reverb should be allowed.

6. Write exclusion logic.
   - State what must not dominate the track.

## If/Then rules
- If the track leans too far into glossy disco, restore reggae pocket through bass placement and offbeat support.
- If the track leans too far into slow roots language, restore dance momentum through kick flow and brighter harmonic motion.
- If the groove feels stiff, loosen the relationship between bass, hats, and offbeat accents.
- If the result feels too pop, reduce obvious chorus writing and strengthen rhythm identity.
- If the result feels too sleepy, increase propulsion without removing depth.
- If the result feels overcrowded, simplify simultaneous rhythmic claims.

## Default disco-reggae core profile
- Hybrid center: Even fusion with slight disco-forward motion
- Groove center: Bassline and offbeat rhythm support
- Energy profile: Warm rolling propulsion
- Harmony color: Sun-warm minor/major blend with soulful brightness
- Spatial stance: Moderate dub depth, not full dub deconstruction
- Emotional tone: Joyful, grounded, communal, late-evening warmth

## Guardrails
- Do not treat this as generic tropical pop.
- Do not erase reggae pocket with over-quantized disco stiffness.
- Do not erase disco lift with overly static roots pacing.
- Avoid fake Caribbean signifiers without groove logic.
- Avoid novelty fusion clichés.
- Avoid EDM language unless explicitly requested.

## Output format
Return:
- Hybrid center
- Groove center
- Energy profile
- Harmonic color
- Spatial stance
- Emotional tone
- Explicit red flags to avoid
