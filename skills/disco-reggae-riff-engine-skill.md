# disco-reggae-riff-engine-skill

## Purpose
Use this skill to define the repeating musical engine of a disco-reggae track.

In this context, “riff” may mean:
- bass hook
- guitar/keys offbeat figure
- chord stab cycle
- vocal phrase loop
- horn response
- synth motif

## When to use
Use this skill when:
- the track needs a memorable repeating center
- the groove exists but no hook is locking it together
- the user wants a stronger bass or motif identity
- the hybrid needs clearer interplay between disco repetition and reggae pocket

## Workflow
1. Choose the hook carrier.
   - bassline
   - offbeat guitar
   - bubble keys
   - chord stab
   - vocal chant
   - horn phrase
   - synth figure

2. Choose the hook function.
   - propulsion
   - sweetness
   - bounce
   - tension
   - crowd memory
   - groove glue

3. Choose phrase length.
   - 1 bar
   - 2 bars
   - 4 bars

4. Choose mutation logic.
   - rhythmic variation
   - filter or tone change
   - octave lift
   - response phrase
   - dub drop-out
   - call and answer

## If/Then rules
- If disco energy is leading, keep the motif more continuous and danceable.
- If reggae pocket is leading, leave more air between statements.
- If the result is too busy, reduce hook density before reducing groove.
- If the track lacks memorability, simplify the bass or phrase shape.
- If the hook sounds too rock-like, rephrase it around syncopation, offbeat support, or bass contour.

## Guardrails
- Avoid guitar-riff dominance unless explicitly requested.
- Avoid too many simultaneous hooks.
- Avoid overplaying every bar.
- Avoid melody shapes that fight the groove.

## Output format
Return:
- Hook carrier
- Hook function
- Phrase length
- Mutation logic
- Interaction with bass and groove
- What to avoid
